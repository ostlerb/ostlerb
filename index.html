Welcome! My name's Bricker. A little about me: I was born and raised near White Bear Lake, MN and graduated from Lawrence University with a mathematics major and minors in German and physics. Currently, I work at Los Alamos National Lab as a post-baccalaureate researcher in particle accelerator physics.

My interests lie at the intersection of mathematics, physics, and computer science; I'm currently applying to computational mathematics and physics graduate schools for Autumn 2020. My research includes computational group-theortic approaches to understanding complex oscillator dynamics and the use of off-axis undulator radiation as a novel current profile diagnostic. At the heart of it all, though, is computation as a method to illuminate physical problems.
